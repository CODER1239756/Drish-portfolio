For Drish, on his birthday

Dear Drish,

There are people who move through the world collecting things — trophies, followers, achievements. And then there are people like you, who move through it quietly building things, and somehow that ends up meaning far more.

You picked up a keyboard and taught yourself to make things appear on screens that did not exist before. You picked up a camera and trained yourself to see light the way most people never learn to. You stepped onto a cricket pitch, a volleyball court, a football ground — and brought the same discipline to each one.

That is not nothing. That is actually extraordinary.

Growing up is an uneven road. Some stretches are harder than they look from the outside. But every line of code you have written, every photograph you have taken, every sprint you have made on the field — all of it has been practice for the version of you that is still becoming.

So as another September 24th arrives, here is what I want you to know:

The curiosity you carry — protect it. The work ethic you have built — keep building. The people you love — show them.

The best commits are still ahead. The best frames are still unshot. The best version of Drish Shah is still loading.

Happy Birthday, Drish.

Keep building things worth keeping.

Written with respect and admiration.
